Introduction

Flaky, tender, and slightly sweet, Cornetti Italiani are the Italian version of croissants that bring a cozy, café-style breakfast to your kitchen with minimal effort.

History

Cornetti have a long tradition in Italian bakeries, especially in Rome and Northern Italy, often enjoyed with espresso for breakfast. They are a lighter, less buttery cousin of the French croissant, adapted for Italian tastes.

Why We Love It

The soft, slightly sweet dough, tender layers, and golden finish make Cornetti a comforting breakfast treat. Whether filled with chocolate, jam, or enjoyed plain, they pair perfectly with a morning espresso or cappuccino.

Ingredients

  • 3 ½ cups (440 g) all-purpose flour
  • ¼ cup (50 g) sugar
  • 2 ¼ tsp (7 g) active dry yeast
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ¾ cup (180 ml) warm milk (about 105°F / 40°C)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract (or zest of 1 lemon)
  • ½ cup (115 g) unsalted butter, softened
  • Optional filling: chocolate chips, jam, or Nutella
  • Topping: 1 egg yolk + 1 tbsp milk, powdered sugar for dusting

Instructions

  1. Activate yeast: Mix warm milk with 1 tsp sugar and yeast. Let stand 5–10 minutes until foamy.
  2. Make the dough: Mix flour, remaining sugar, salt, eggs, vanilla, and yeast mixture. Knead 8–10 minutes until smooth.
  3. Add butter gradually, kneading until fully incorporated. Dough becomes soft and elastic.
  4. First rise: Place in oiled bowl, cover, let rise 1–1.5 hours until doubled.
  5. Shape croissants: Roll dough into a large circle, cut into 8–10 triangles, add filling if desired, and roll to form crescent shapes.
  6. Second rise: Place on parchment-lined tray, cover, let rise 30–40 minutes.
  7. Bake: Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Brush with egg wash, bake 15–18 minutes until golden.
  8. Finish: Cool slightly, dust with powdered sugar, and serve.

Tips & Tricks

  • Use room-temperature butter to ensure easy incorporation.
  • For extra flavor, add lemon zest or vanilla.
  • Fill cornetti lightly to prevent leaking during baking.

Serving Suggestions

Serve warm with espresso, cappuccino, or hot chocolate — perfect for a cozy Italian breakfast or brunch.

Nutrition Info (per serving)

Approximate: 250 kcal, 9g fat, 35g carbs, 6g protein (per cornetto, no filling).

Fun Fact

Cornetti in Italy are traditionally eaten for breakfast, often paired with a cappuccino. The word "cornetto" means "little horn," referencing the crescent shape.
