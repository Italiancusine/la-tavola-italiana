Introduction

Chicken Cacciatore is a hearty and flavorful Italian dish that means “hunter’s chicken.” This rustic meal features chicken simmered in a savory tomato sauce with bell peppers, onions, garlic, and herbs — perfect for a cozy night in or a family gathering.

History

Originating in central Italy, "cacciatore" was a meal made by hunters after returning from a long day. Traditionally made with game meat, modern versions use chicken or rabbit, braised slowly in tomatoes, herbs, and sometimes olives or wine.

Why We Love It

This one-pan wonder is packed with Italian flavor, easy to make, and tastes even better the next day. It's gluten-free, customizable, and absolutely satisfying with pasta, polenta, or crusty bread.

Ingredients

  • 4 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 can (14 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup dry red wine (optional)
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1/2 tsp dried thyme
  • Salt & pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley or basil, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Season chicken with salt and pepper. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium-high heat.
  2. Sear chicken thighs skin-side down until golden brown (about 5 minutes per side). Remove and set aside.
  3. In the same pan, sauté onions, peppers, and garlic until softened (about 5–7 minutes).
  4. Add crushed tomatoes, wine (if using), oregano, and thyme. Stir and bring to a simmer.
  5. Return chicken to the pan, skin-side up. Cover and simmer on low heat for 30–35 minutes or until chicken is cooked through and tender.
  6. Uncover and simmer 5 more minutes to thicken the sauce. Taste and adjust seasoning.
  7. Serve hot with fresh herbs and your favorite side dish.

Tips & Tricks

  • Use boneless thighs or drumsticks if you prefer.
  • Make it spicy with red pepper flakes.
  • Double the sauce for serving over pasta or rice.
  • Great for meal prep — it tastes even better the next day!

Serving Suggestions

Serve Chicken Cacciatore with polenta, mashed potatoes, spaghetti, or rustic bread. A green salad and a glass of Chianti complete the meal beautifully.

Fun Fact

“Cacciatore” means “hunter” in Italian. The dish is named after how hunters would prepare game meat using simple, local ingredients from the forest and garden.
