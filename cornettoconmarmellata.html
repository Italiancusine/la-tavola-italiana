Introduction

The Cornetto con Marmellata is a classic Italian breakfast pastry — a golden, flaky, buttery croissant delicately filled with luscious, sweet, fruity jam. Perfectly paired with a rich espresso or creamy cappuccino for an indulgent morning treat.

History

Cornetti are the authentic Italian counterpart to the French croissant, featuring a lighter, airy dough with a subtly sweet flavor. Traditionally enjoyed at breakfast, these pastries are often filled with vibrant fruit jams or smooth creams, reflecting the artisanal Italian café culture.

Why We Love It

Experience the perfect balance of crispy, golden outer layers and soft, flavorful, melt-in-your-mouth filling. Comforting, aromatic, and delightfully indulgent, these pastries bring the charm of Italian cafés directly to your kitchen.

Ingredients

  • 1 package of store-bought or freshly prepared, tender croissant dough
  • 1/2 cup fruity, aromatic marmalade or jam (apricot, raspberry, strawberry)
  • 1 fresh egg (for glossy egg wash)
  • Powdered sugar, optional for a delicate finish

Instructions

  1. Prepare baking sheet, parchment paper, egg wash, brush, and knife.
  2. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C) for golden, flaky results.
  3. Unroll dough, separate triangles, add 1 tbsp of fragrant, sweet marmalade to each.
  4. Roll triangles into classic cornetto shapes, gently curving the ends.
  5. Place on baking sheet and brush generously with egg wash.
  6. Bake 18–22 minutes until perfectly puffed, golden, and slightly crisp.
  7. Cool slightly, dust with powdered sugar, serve warm with a café-style beverage.

Tips & Tricks

  • Keep dough chilled until ready to roll to ensure flaky layers.
  • Use high-quality jam for the best flavor contrast.
  • Let baked cornetti cool slightly to prevent filling from spilling out.

Serving Suggestions

Pair your Cornetto con Marmellata with a cappuccino or espresso, a fresh fruit salad, and a light morning spread for the perfect Italian breakfast experience.

Nutrition Info (per serving)

Approximate: 250 kcal, 10g fat, 35g carbs, 4g protein.

Fun Fact

In Italy, cornetti are typically enjoyed for breakfast, often with just a cappuccino, as a daily indulgence rather than a dessert.
