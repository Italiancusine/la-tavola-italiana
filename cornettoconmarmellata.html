Introduction

The Cornetto con Marmellata is a classic Italian breakfast pastry — a flaky, buttery croissant filled with sweet, fruity jam. It’s a simple yet delightful treat that pairs perfectly with your morning coffee or cappuccino.

History

Cornetti are the Italian version of the French croissant, with a lighter, less buttery dough. Typically enjoyed at breakfast in Italy, they are often filled with various jams or creams, making them a versatile and beloved staple of Italian cafés.

Why We Love It

We love Cornetto con Marmellata for its perfect balance of crispy outer layers and soft, flavorful filling. It’s quick to enjoy, comforting, and brings a touch of Italian café culture right into your kitchen.

Ingredients

  • 1 package of store-bought croissant dough (or homemade croissant dough)
  • 1/2 cup of your favorite fruit marmalade or jam (apricot, raspberry, or strawberry work great)
  • 1 egg (for egg wash)
  • Powdered sugar, for dusting (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare your tools: baking sheet, parchment paper, small bowl for egg wash, pastry brush, and a knife.
  2. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and line the baking sheet with parchment paper.
  3. Unroll the croissant dough and separate into triangles.
  4. Place about 1 tablespoon of marmalade at the wide end of each triangle.
  5. Roll the dough from the wide end to the tip, forming the cornetto shape. Curve the ends slightly inward if you like.
  6. Place the rolled cornetti on the baking sheet, spacing them evenly.
  7. Beat the egg in a small bowl, then brush the tops of each cornetto with the egg wash to get a beautiful golden finish.
  8. Bake for 12–15 minutes or until golden brown and puffed.
  9. Remove from oven and let cool for a few minutes.
  10. Optional: dust with powdered sugar before serving.

Tips & Tricks

  • For an even flakier texture, chill the dough for 15 minutes before baking.
  • Don’t overfill with marmalade to prevent leaking during baking.
  • Try using different jams or add a small piece of chocolate for variation.

Serving Suggestions

Enjoy your Cornetto con Marmellata warm with a rich espresso or creamy cappuccino. It’s perfect for a leisurely Italian-style breakfast or a cozy afternoon snack.

Fun Fact

The word "cornetto" literally means "little horn," referring to the crescent shape of the pastry.
