Introduction

The Cornetto con Marmellata is a classic Italian breakfast pastry — a flaky, buttery croissant filled with sweet, fruity jam. Perfect with morning coffee or cappuccino.

History

Cornetti are the Italian version of the French croissant, with a lighter, less buttery dough. Enjoyed at breakfast, often filled with various jams or creams.

Why We Love It

Perfect balance of crispy outer layers and soft, flavorful filling. Comforting and brings Italian café culture to your kitchen.

Ingredients

  • 1 package of store-bought croissant dough (or homemade)
  • 1/2 cup fruit marmalade or jam (apricot, raspberry, strawberry)
  • 1 egg (for egg wash)
  • Powdered sugar, optional

Instructions

  1. Prepare baking sheet, parchment, egg wash, brush, knife.
  2. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  3. Unroll dough, separate triangles, add 1 tbsp marmalade each.
  4. Roll triangles to form cornetto, curve ends slightly.
  5. Place on baking sheet, brush with egg wash.
  6. Bake 12–15 min until golden.
  7. Cool briefly, dust with powdered sugar if desired.

Tips & Tricks

  • Chill dough 15 minutes before baking for flakier texture.
  • Don't overfill to prevent leaks.
  • Add chocolate or try different jams for variation.

Serving Suggestions

Enjoy warm with espresso or cappuccino. Great for Italian-style breakfast or cozy snack.

Nutrition Info (per serving)

  • Calories: 250
  • Protein: 4g
  • Carbohydrates: 30g
  • Fat: 12g
  • Fiber: 1g
  • Sodium: 180mg

Fun Fact

"Cornetto" literally means "little horn," referring to the crescent shape.
