Italian Garlic Knots
Knots all'aglio italiani

Soft, golden, and bursting with garlicky flavor, these Italian Garlic Knots are the perfect appetizer or side. They pair beautifully with marinara sauce or a light drizzle of olive oil.

Ingredients

  • 1 batch of pizza dough (makes 12–16 knots)
  • 3 tbsp olive oil or melted butter (or a mix)
  • 3 cloves garlic, finely minced or pressed
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley, finely chopped (or 1 tsp dried parsley)
  • ¼ cup grated Parmesan cheese (optional, for topping)
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven: Set to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Shape the knots: After dough has risen, divide into 12–16 pieces. Roll each into a 6-inch rope, tie into a loose knot, tuck ends underneath, and place on baking sheet leaving space between.
  3. First bake: Bake for 12–15 minutes until golden and puffed.
  4. Make garlic oil/butter: While baking, heat olive oil or butter in a small pan. Add garlic and sauté 30–60 seconds until fragrant. Stir in parsley and salt, then remove from heat.
  5. Coat the knots: Brush baked knots generously with garlic mixture. Sprinkle with Parmesan if using.
  6. Serve warm: Best eaten fresh, with marinara sauce for dipping.

✨ Variations to Make It Special

  • Cheesy Garlic Knots: Add mozzarella cubes inside before tying the dough.
  • Spicy Knots: Add a pinch of red pepper flakes to the garlic butter.
  • Herbed Knots: Mix Italian herbs (oregano, basil, thyme) into dough or butter.
  • Sweet Dessert Knots: Brush baked knots with melted butter and cinnamon sugar instead of garlic for a kid-friendly twist.
