Introduction

Risotto alla Milanese is a luxurious, creamy Italian rice dish infused with fragrant saffron and rich Parmesan cheese. This iconic dish from Milan combines simple ingredients to create deep, comforting flavors that have delighted food lovers for centuries.

History

The origins of Risotto alla Milanese trace back to 16th-century Milan. Legend has it that a young apprentice accidentally dropped saffron into a risotto pot during a wedding feast, creating the signature golden color and distinct aroma that quickly became a local sensation.

Why We Love It

This risotto boasts a beautiful golden hue and a delicate balance between the creamy texture of the rice and the floral, earthy notes of saffron. It's elegant enough for special occasions but simple enough for a cozy weeknight dinner.

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ cups Arborio rice
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth, kept warm
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 3 tbsp unsalted butter, divided
  • ½ cup dry white wine
  • 1/2 tsp high-quality saffron threads
  • 1/2 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Heat the broth in a saucepan and keep it warm over low heat.
  2. In a large pan, melt 2 tbsp butter and sauté the onion until translucent.
  3. Add the Arborio rice and stir to coat the grains with butter; toast for 2 minutes.
  4. Pour in the white wine and cook until mostly evaporated.
  5. Steep the saffron threads in a small bowl with 2 tbsp warm broth for 5 minutes, then add to the rice.
  6. Add warm broth one ladle at a time, stirring constantly until absorbed before adding more.
  7. Continue for about 18-20 minutes, until the rice is creamy and al dente.
  8. Remove from heat; stir in remaining butter and Parmesan cheese. Season with salt and pepper.
  9. Serve immediately, garnished with extra Parmesan if desired.

Tips & Tricks

  • Keep broth warm throughout to ensure even cooking.
  • Use high-quality saffron for the best flavor and color.
  • Stir frequently but gently to release the rice’s starch and create creaminess.
  • For a vegetarian version, use vegetable broth.

Serving Suggestions

Pair Risotto alla Milanese with a crisp white wine like Pinot Grigio and a simple green salad to balance the rich flavors.
