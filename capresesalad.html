Caprese Salad

Introduction

Caprese Salad celebrates the essence of Italian simplicity — fresh tomatoes, creamy mozzarella, vibrant basil, and a drizzle of extra-virgin olive oil. Bright, refreshing, and elegant, it’s a timeless classic.

History

This dish originated in Capri, Italy, and was designed to showcase the colors of the Italian flag: red, white, and green. Over time, it has become one of the most iconic dishes in Mediterranean cuisine.

Why We Love It

It requires no cooking, uses minimal ingredients, and delivers perfect balance: sweet tomatoes, rich cheese, aromatic basil, and silky olive oil.

Ingredients

  • 3 ripe tomatoes, sliced
  • 8 oz fresh mozzarella, sliced
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • Balsamic glaze (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Arrange the tomato and mozzarella slices alternately on a plate.
  2. Tuck fresh basil leaves between the slices.
  3. Drizzle generously with olive oil.
  4. Season with salt and black pepper.
  5. Finish with balsamic glaze if desired.

Tips & Tricks

  • Use room-temperature tomatoes for maximum flavor.
  • Choose high-quality olive oil — it makes a difference.
  • Add flaky salt for a gourmet finish.
  • Try heirloom tomatoes for a colorful twist.

Serving Suggestions

Serve as an appetizer, a light lunch, or alongside grilled chicken or pasta. Pair with sparkling water or crisp white wine.

Nutrition Info (Per Serving)

  • Calories: 210
  • Carbs: 6g
  • Protein: 15g
  • Fat: 14g
  • Sodium: 300mg

Fun Fact

Because Caprese celebrates the Italian flag colors, it’s sometimes called “the patriotic salad” in Italy!
