Introduction

Tiramisu is a beloved Italian dessert featuring layers of coffee-soaked ladyfingers and rich mascarpone cream. It's the perfect balance of bitter espresso, sweet cream, and a dusting of cocoa.

History

Originating in the Veneto region of Italy in the 1960s, tiramisu literally means "pick me up"—a nod to its energizing espresso and uplifting flavor. It quickly became popular across Italy and the world.

Why We Love It

Tiramisu is elegant yet easy to make. It requires no baking and delivers a gourmet experience in every bite, making it a go-to for dinner parties or indulgent weekends.

Ingredients

  • 6 egg yolks
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup mascarpone cheese
  • 1 1/2 cups heavy cream
  • 2 cups strong brewed coffee, cooled
  • 1/4 cup coffee liqueur (optional)
  • 1 package ladyfingers
  • Unsweetened cocoa powder for dusting

Instructions

  1. Whisk egg yolks and sugar in a bowl over simmering water until thick. Cool slightly.
  2. Fold in mascarpone cheese until smooth.
  3. In a separate bowl, whip heavy cream until stiff peaks form. Fold into mascarpone mixture.
  4. Combine coffee and liqueur. Dip ladyfingers briefly into mixture and layer in dish.
  5. Spread half the mascarpone mixture over ladyfingers. Repeat with remaining layers.
  6. Dust with cocoa powder. Chill at least 4 hours or overnight.

Tips & Tricks

  • Use cold, strong coffee for best flavor.
  • Don’t oversoak ladyfingers—just a quick dip!
  • Let tiramisu chill overnight for best texture and taste.

Serving Suggestions

Serve tiramisu cold, cut into squares with an extra dusting of cocoa or grated chocolate. Pairs well with espresso or dessert wine.
