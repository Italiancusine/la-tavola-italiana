Tiramisu

Jump
Facebook Twitter WhatsApp Email

Introduction

Tiramisu is a beloved Italian dessert made of espresso-soaked ladyfingers layered with a silky mascarpone cream. Its rich flavor and velvety texture make it a timeless favorite.

History

This classic dessert originated in the Veneto region in the 1960s. Its name means "pick me up," inspired by the energizing espresso combined with luscious sweetness.

Why We Love It

No baking required, elegant presentation, and a perfect balance of flavors — tiramisu is ideal for celebrations or weekend treats.

Ingredients

  • 6 egg yolks
  • 3/4 cup sugar
  • 1 cup mascarpone
  • 1 1/2 cups heavy cream
  • 2 cups strong coffee, cooled
  • 1/4 cup coffee liqueur (optional)
  • 1 package ladyfingers
  • Cocoa powder

Instructions

  1. Whisk egg yolks and sugar over simmering water until thick. Let cool.
  2. Add mascarpone and mix until smooth.
  3. Whip heavy cream to stiff peaks and fold in.
  4. Dip ladyfingers briefly in coffee (and liqueur).
  5. Layer soaked ladyfingers and mascarpone cream.
  6. Dust with cocoa and chill 4+ hours.

Tips & Tricks

  • Use cold, strong espresso.
  • Dip ladyfingers quickly — don’t over-soak.
  • Chill overnight for the very best flavor.

Serving Suggestions

Serve cold with extra cocoa or shaved chocolate. Pairs beautifully with espresso.

Nutrition (per serving)

Calories: 410 | Fat: 28g | Protein: 6g

Fun Fact

The name “Tiramisu” literally means “pick me up,” referring to the energizing espresso within the layers.
