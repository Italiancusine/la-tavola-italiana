Introduction

These Unique Italian Biga Ciabatta Rolls bring the authentic Italian artisan bread experience to your kitchen. Golden crusts, airy interiors, and subtle herbal aromas make these rolls perfect for breakfast, sandwiches, or a rustic table centerpiece.

History

Ciabatta, meaning “slipper” in Italian, was created in the 1980s as a modern Italian response to French bread. Using a biga or preferment helps develop deep flavor and an open, soft crumb that’s highly prized in traditional Italian bakeries.

Why We Love It

The combination of a fragrant biga, wet and sticky dough, and gentle folding creates perfectly airy rolls with a crisp crust. The process may seem intricate, but the results are worth every step — artisan bread made at home.

Ingredients

Biga / Preferment

  • 1 cup (130g) bread flour or white spelt flour
  • 1/8 tsp active-dry or instant yeast
  • 1/2 cup (120ml) water, room temperature
  • Optional: 1 tsp fresh rosemary or ½ tsp dried thyme

Ciabatta Dough

  • 1 tsp active-dry or instant yeast
  • 1 cup (240ml) warm water (100–110°F / 38–43°C)
  • 2 1/2 cups (325g) bread flour or white spelt flour
  • 1 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp olive oil (optional)
  • Optional: 1–2 tbsp sun-dried tomatoes, olives, or caramelized onion

Instructions

  1. Biga / Preferment: Mix flour, yeast, and herbs. Add water, cover, rest 8–24 hours until lightly fermented.
  2. Ciabatta Dough: Dissolve yeast in warm water, add biga, flour, salt, olive oil, and optional mix-ins. Mix gently until no dry flour remains.
  3. First rest 30 min, then perform stretch & folds: fold 12→6 o’clock, rotate 90°, repeat around bowl (8 folds). Repeat 3 more times, resting 30 min between sets. Optionally drizzle olive oil on top before each fold.
  4. Cold ferment 1–24 hours in refrigerator after 4th fold.
  5. Shape: flour surface, gently scrape dough, optionally sprinkle semolina or cornmeal, form 8 rolls (or 2 loaves), place 2 inches apart, cover, rest 1 hour.
  6. Bake: preheat oven 450°F (232°C) with sheet inside, add ice cubes for steam, bake 20–25 min until golden and hollow-sounding. Optional: brush tops with olive oil immediately.

Tips & Tricks

  • Keep dough wet and handle gently to preserve air pockets.
  • Use high-quality flour for best flavor and texture.
  • Flavor variations: herbed, olive & tomato, cheesy, or nutty additions.

Serving Suggestions

Enjoy warm with butter, olive oil, or as a base for sandwiches. Perfect for brunch spreads or alongside soups and salads.

Nutrition Info (per serving)

Approximate: 180–220 kcal, 3–5g fat, 35–40g carbs, 6–8g protein (per roll).

Fun Fact

Ciabatta literally means "slipper" in Italian, named for its shape. Its airy interior comes from a very wet dough, unlike most other bread doughs.
