Introduction

Lasagna is the ultimate comfort food and a symbol of Italian culinary tradition. Layers of tender pasta sheets, rich meat sauce, creamy béchamel, and melted cheese combine to create a hearty dish loved worldwide.

History

Lasagna has roots in Ancient Rome, but the modern layered version comes from Italy’s Emilia-Romagna region. The word "lasagna" originally referred to the pot it was cooked in!

Why We Love It

It’s filling, rich, deeply flavored, and perfect for sharing. The blend of tomato sauce, cheese, and pasta is hard to resist and even better the next day.

Ingredients

  • 12 lasagna noodles
  • 1 lb (450g) ground beef or beef/pork mix
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp oregano
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 cups ricotta cheese
  • 2 cups mozzarella, shredded
  • 1 cup Parmesan, grated
  • 3 cups béchamel sauce
  • Fresh basil (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Cook noodles until al dente. Set aside.
  2. Sauté onion and garlic, then brown the meat. Add tomatoes, paste, and spices. Simmer 20–30 mins.
  3. Layer meat sauce, noodles, ricotta, béchamel, mozzarella, and Parmesan. Repeat.
  4. Cover and bake 25 mins. Uncover and bake 20 more until golden.
  5. Rest 10 mins before serving. Garnish with basil if desired.

Tips & Tricks

  • Don’t skip the béchamel — it adds creamy richness.
  • Make ahead and reheat for deeper flavor.
  • Freeze in portions for easy weeknight meals.

Serving Suggestions

Pair with a crisp salad and a glass of Chianti. Finish with gelato or espresso.
