Torta di Miele
Italian Honey Cake

This classic Italian honey cake is sweet, fragrant, and perfect with a cup of tea or coffee. Its rustic, golden crumb makes it a comforting dessert for any occasion.

History

Torta di Miele is a traditional Italian cake often prepared in Tuscany and other regions where honey is abundant. It is celebrated for its simplicity, natural sweetness, and ability to showcase high-quality honey flavors.

Why We Love It

We love this cake for its moist, tender crumb and the way honey naturally enhances flavor. It’s simple enough for weeknight baking but special enough to impress guests. Plus, it pairs wonderfully with fresh fruit, coffee, or dessert wine.

Ingredients (Base Recipe)

  • 2 cups (250 g) all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • Pinch of salt
  • ½ cup (115 g) unsalted butter, melted (or olive oil for a rustic touch)
  • ¾ cup (250 g) honey (good quality, preferably floral or acacia)
  • ½ cup (100 g) sugar (can use brown sugar for deeper flavor)
  • 3 large eggs
  • ½ cup (120 ml) milk (or buttermilk for tenderness)
  • Zest of 1 lemon or orange
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 tsp cinnamon (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour a 9-inch (23 cm) round cake pan or springform.
  2. In one bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, and cinnamon.
  3. In another bowl, whisk honey, sugar, eggs, melted butter, milk, zest, and vanilla until smooth.
  4. Fold dry ingredients into wet until just combined.
  5. Pour batter into prepared pan and bake 30–35 minutes, until golden and a toothpick comes out clean.
  6. Cool slightly, then drizzle with extra warm honey before serving.

✨ Variations to Make It Special

  • Nutty Delight: Fold in ½ cup chopped toasted almonds, walnuts, or hazelnuts. Sprinkle slivered almonds on top before baking.
  • Citrus Sunshine: Use both lemon and orange zest. Make a glaze with honey + fresh lemon juice and drizzle over the cooled cake.
  • Tuscan Spice: Add ½ tsp each of cinnamon, nutmeg, and cloves. Great for autumn or holidays — similar to Tuscan panforte flavors, but lighter.
  • Fruit Lover’s Cake: Stir in ½ cup golden raisins, chopped dried figs, or dates soaked in warm tea. Top with fresh figs or berries before serving.
  • Creamy Nonna’s Version: Slice cooled cake in half and fill with whipped cream or mascarpone sweetened with honey. Dust with powdered sugar.
  • Gluten-Free Rustic: Replace flour with almond flour (1½ cups) + cornstarch (½ cup). Texture becomes moist, almost like a torte.
  • Holiday Struffoli Twist: Bake cake, then drizzle with warm honey mixed with candied orange peel and colorful sprinkles, inspired by Neapolitan struffoli.

Tips & Tricks

  • Use high-quality honey for the best flavor.
  • For a more rustic texture, use a wooden spoon to fold ingredients instead of a mixer.
  • Allow cake to cool slightly before glazing with honey to avoid soaking the crumb too much.

Serving Suggestions

Pair with espresso, vin santo, or a simple sweet tea. Garnish with fresh berries or a light dusting of powdered sugar for presentation.

Nutrition Info (per serving — estimated)

  • Cholesterol: ~80 mg

  • Carbohydrates: ~45 g

Fun Fact

The cake’s sweetness comes entirely from honey, a natural preservative, making Torta di Miele a traditional favorite for festive gatherings in Italy.
