Panini Caprese

Introduction

Panini Caprese is a grilled Italian sandwich inspired by the classic Caprese salad. Fresh mozzarella, juicy tomatoes, and fragrant basil are layered on rustic bread and grilled to golden perfection — warm, melty, and packed with Mediterranean flavor.

History

The Caprese salad originated on the Isle of Capri and became a symbol of Italian simplicity and freshness. Turning it into a panini is a more modern twist, popularized in cafés and homes across Italy for a warm version of this iconic dish.

Why We Love It

We love this recipe because it’s quick, easy, and made with just a few fresh ingredients. It’s vegetarian, satisfying, and perfect for lunch, dinner, or even a hearty snack.

Ingredients

  • 2 slices of ciabatta or sourdough bread
  • 3–4 slices of fresh mozzarella
  • 3 slices of ripe tomato
  • 4 fresh basil leaves
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Balsamic glaze (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat a panini press or skillet over medium heat.
  2. Brush the outside of both bread slices lightly with olive oil.
  3. Layer mozzarella, tomatoes, and basil between the slices of bread.
  4. Season with salt and pepper and drizzle with balsamic glaze if using.
  5. Grill the sandwich in a panini press or on the skillet, pressing down gently, for 3–5 minutes per side or until golden and the cheese is melted.
  6. Remove, slice, and serve warm.

Tips & Tricks

  • Use day-old bread for extra crispiness.
  • Add a thin slice of prosciutto if you’d like a non-vegetarian version.
  • Don’t skip the salt — it brings out the flavor of the tomatoes and cheese.

Serving Suggestions

Serve with a light arugula salad or a small bowl of soup. A sparkling water with lemon or a chilled iced tea pairs beautifully for a complete lunch.

Fun Fact

The colors in a Caprese — red (tomato), white (mozzarella), and green (basil) — reflect the Italian flag!
