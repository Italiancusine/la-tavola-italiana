Introduction

Bruschetta is a beloved Italian appetizer that showcases vibrant flavors and rustic charm. With its crispy grilled bread topped with fresh tomatoes, garlic, basil, and olive oil, it's a delightful start to any Italian meal.

History

Bruschetta dates back to ancient Rome when olive oil producers tasted their freshly pressed oil using slices of grilled bread. The name comes from "bruscare," a Roman dialect word meaning "to roast over coals."

Ingredients

  • 1 rustic Italian bread or baguette
  • 4 ripe tomatoes, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 garlic clove (for rubbing toast)
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Slice the bread and toast or grill until golden and crisp.
  2. Rub each slice with a garlic clove while still warm.
  3. In a bowl, mix tomatoes, minced garlic, basil, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  4. Spoon the mixture onto the bread just before serving to avoid sogginess.
  5. Drizzle with additional olive oil and serve immediately.

Serving Suggestions

Serve bruschetta as a pre-dinner antipasto or as part of an appetizer spread with olives, cheeses, and cured meats. Pair with a crisp white wine like Verdicchio or a sparkling Prosecco.
