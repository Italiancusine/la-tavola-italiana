Ingredients

  • 2 lbs whole milk ricotta cheese (well drained)
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 tsp pure vanilla extract
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup cornstarch
  • Pinch of salt
  • Powdered sugar (for garnish, optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350¬∞F (175¬∞C). Grease a 9-inch springform pan and line the bottom with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk ricotta cheese to smooth out any lumps.
  3. Add sugar, eggs, vanilla, lemon zest, and lemon juice. Mix well until combined.
  4. Sift cornstarch and salt into the mixture. Stir gently until fully incorporated.
  5. Pour batter into prepared pan and smooth the top.
  6. Bake for 50-60 minutes or until the edges are set and the center is just slightly jiggly.
  7. Turn off oven, crack the door open, and let cheesecake cool inside for 1 hour to prevent cracking.
  8. Remove from oven, cool completely at room temperature, then refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight.
  9. Before serving, dust with powdered sugar if desired.

Tips & Tricks

  • Make sure your ricotta is well-drained to avoid a watery cheesecake.
  • Use room temperature eggs for better mixing and texture.
  • Don‚Äôt overbake; the cheesecake should still have a slight wobble when done.
  • Let the cheesecake cool slowly in the oven to avoid cracks.
  • For a crust, try a simple almond or biscotti crumb base (optional).

Fun Fact

The word "ricotta" means "recooked" in Italian, referring to the traditional process of making ricotta cheese by reheating the whey left over from other cheeses. This process yields the soft, creamy cheese that makes this cheesecake so uniquely delicious!
