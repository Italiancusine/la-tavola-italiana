Introduction

Italian-Style Cornflake Schnitzel brings the fun crunch of cornflakes together with classic Italian herb flavors — golden, crispy, and deeply satisfying. It’s a quick, family-friendly main that feels both familiar and a little bit special.

History

While the breaded cutlet is best known as the Austrian Wiener Schnitzel, Italy’s long tradition of breaded meats (cotoletta alla milanese) likely predates it. The idea of using a crunchy coating has evolved everywhere — this variation uses cornflakes for an extra-light crisp and Italian herbs to give the dish Mediterranean character.

Why We Love It

We love this version because it combines three great things: texture, speed, and flavor. The cornflake crumb gives an irresistible crunch that stays crisp after frying; the herb-seasoned breadcrumb mix adds Italian warmth and aroma; and the technique is fast — perfect for weeknights when you want something both comforting and slightly elevated. Kids love the crunch, adults love the seasoning, and everyone loves how it turns simple pantry staples into a memorable meal.

Ingredients (4 servings)

  • 4 chicken cutlets (boneless, skinless, pounded thin)
  • 2 cups cornflakes, crushed into fine crumbs
  • ½ cup breadcrumbs (Italian seasoned, or plain with herbs added)
  • ½ cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tbsp milk or water
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • ½ tsp paprika
  • Salt & black pepper to taste
  • Olive oil or vegetable oil, for frying (about ¼ inch in skillet)

Instructions

  1. Place flour in one shallow bowl.
  2. In a second bowl, whisk eggs with milk.
  3. In a third bowl, mix crushed cornflakes, breadcrumbs, garlic powder, oregano, basil, paprika, salt, and pepper.
  4. Dredge each chicken cutlet first in flour (shake off excess), then dip in egg wash, and finally coat well in the cornflake mixture. Press crumbs gently so they stick.
  5. Heat about ¼ inch oil in a large skillet over medium heat until shimmering (but not smoking).
  6. Fry schnitzels 3–4 minutes per side, until golden brown and cooked through (internal temp 165°F / 74°C).
  7. Drain on paper towels, season immediately with a light pinch of salt, and serve hot with lemon wedges.

Tips & Tricks

  • Use slightly stale or crushed cornflakes for better adhesion — pulse in a food processor for a fine crumb.
  • Don't overcrowd the pan: fry in batches to preserve oil temperature and crispness.
  • For extra flavor, add a tablespoon of grated Parmesan to the cornflake mix.
  • If coating slips off, let cutlets rest 5 minutes after breading so crumbs set before frying.

Serving Suggestions

Serve with lemon wedges, a simple tomato-mozzarella salad (caprese-style), roasted garlic potatoes, or a small bowl of warm marinara for dipping. Garnish with chopped parsley for color and freshness.

Fun Fact

The word “schnitzel” comes from the German diminutive for “slice” — but people across Europe have been breading and frying thin slices of meat for centuries. Using crushed cereal (like cornflakes) for breading became popular in home kitchens because it gives consistent crunch and is a great pantry shortcut.
