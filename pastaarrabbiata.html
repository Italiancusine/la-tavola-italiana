Introduction

Pasta Arrabbiata is a fiery Roman classic made with simple ingredients: garlic, olive oil, tomatoes, and red chili flakes. This bold and spicy dish is the perfect comfort food for heat lovers and pasta enthusiasts alike.

History

The name “arrabbiata” means “angry” in Italian — a nod to the spice of the dish. It originated in Rome during the mid-20th century and quickly became a staple in trattorias across Italy, prized for its minimal ingredients and bold flavor.

Why We Love It

It’s spicy, garlicky, and deeply satisfying. With only a few pantry staples, you can whip up a restaurant-worthy meal in minutes. Plus, it's vegan and budget-friendly.

Ingredients

  • 12 oz (340g) penne or spaghetti
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, finely sliced
  • 1/2–1 teaspoon red pepper flakes (to taste)
  • 1 (14 oz) can crushed tomatoes
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh parsley or basil, chopped (optional)
  • Grated pecorino or parmesan (optional)

Instructions

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and cook pasta according to package instructions. Reserve 1/2 cup pasta water before draining.
  2. Meanwhile, in a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat.
  3. Add garlic and sauté until fragrant but not browned, about 30 seconds.
  4. Stir in red pepper flakes and cook for another 10 seconds.
  5. Pour in crushed tomatoes and bring to a simmer. Cook for 8–10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  6. Season with salt to taste and thin with reserved pasta water if needed.
  7. Add the drained pasta and toss to coat evenly in the sauce.
  8. Serve immediately, garnished with herbs and cheese if desired.

Tips & Tricks

  • Use high-quality canned tomatoes for best flavor.
  • Add more chili flakes if you like it extra spicy — Arrabbiata should have a kick!
  • Top with olives or sautéed mushrooms for variation.

Serving Suggestions

Pair with crusty bread and a crisp green salad. A glass of Italian red like Chianti or Montepulciano complements the spice beautifully.
