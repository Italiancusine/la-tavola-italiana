Margherita Pizza

Introduction

The Margherita Pizza is a symbol of Italian culinary simplicity and elegance. Featuring a thin, crisp crust, fresh tomato sauce, creamy mozzarella, and fragrant basil, it captures the essence of Naples in every bite. It's the perfect example of how a few quality ingredients can create an unforgettable dish.

History

The Margherita Pizza was created in Naples in 1889 to honor Queen Margherita of Savoy. Chef Raffaele Esposito topped a pizza with tomatoes, mozzarella, and basil to represent the colors of the Italian flag. The Queen was so impressed, the dish was named after her, and it has since become a staple of Italian cuisine worldwide.

Why We Love It

This pizza is a masterpiece of simplicity and balance. The fresh ingredients shine individually and together, creating a harmony of flavors that is both comforting and sophisticated. It's a great introduction to authentic Italian cooking and a timeless favorite enjoyed worldwide.

Ingredients

  • 1 ball of pizza dough (about 250g)
  • 1/2 cup crushed San Marzano tomatoes
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 125g (4 oz) fresh mozzarella, torn
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 500°F (260°C) and place a pizza stone or inverted baking sheet inside to heat.
  2. Roll out the dough on a floured surface into a thin 10-12 inch circle.
  3. Spread the crushed tomatoes evenly over the dough, leaving a small border for the crust.
  4. Drizzle with olive oil and lightly season with salt.
  5. Add the mozzarella pieces evenly across the surface.
  6. Carefully transfer the pizza to the hot stone and bake for 7-10 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbling.
  7. Remove from the oven and scatter fresh basil leaves on top before serving.

Tips & Tricks

  • Use a pizza stone or steel to replicate the intense heat of a pizzeria oven.
  • Drain the mozzarella slightly if it's too wet to avoid a soggy crust.
  • For an even crispier bottom, try pre-baking the crust for 2-3 minutes before adding toppings.
  • Never overload the pizza—less is more in a true Margherita.
  • Add basil after baking to keep it fresh and flavorful.

Serving Suggestions

Serve your Margherita Pizza hot with a drizzle of extra virgin olive oil and a sprinkle of sea salt. Pair it with a fresh arugula salad and a glass of Chianti or a light Italian lager. It also makes a fantastic appetizer when sliced into small wedges for sharing.

The Margherita Pizza’s colors—red, white, and green—were chosen to represent the Italian flag, making it not just delicious but also a patriotic dish!
