Creamy Mushroom Risotto

Introduction

This creamy mushroom risotto is the ultimate comfort food, combining rich flavors of sautéed mushrooms, fragrant Arborio rice, and a touch of Parmesan. It’s perfect for a cozy dinner or special occasion.

History

Risotto has its origins in Northern Italy, especially the Lombardy region, and dates back to the 14th century. Traditionally, it was a humble peasant dish, but over centuries it has become a symbol of Italian culinary excellence.

Why We Love It

We love this mushroom risotto for its velvety texture, deep umami flavors, and versatility. It’s elegant enough for guests yet easy enough for a weeknight meal.

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 300g (10 oz) mushrooms (button, cremini, or a mix), sliced
  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine (optional)
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth, kept warm
  • 1/2 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)
  • Optional: A squeeze of lemon juice for brightness

Instructions

  1. Sauté onion in olive oil until softened; add garlic and cook 1 min.
  2. Add mushrooms and cook until golden brown, about 5–7 minutes.
  3. Stir in Arborio rice and toast for 2–3 minutes.
  4. Pour in white wine (if using) and stir until mostly absorbed.
  5. Add warm broth one ladle at a time, stirring until absorbed; repeat for ~20 mins until rice is creamy and al dente.
  6. Stir in butter and Parmesan; season with salt and pepper.
  7. Serve garnished with parsley and a squeeze of lemon if desired.

Tips & Tricks

  • Keep broth warm to speed up cooking and improve texture.
  • Stir frequently but not constantly for perfect creaminess.
  • Use mixed mushrooms like shiitake or porcini for richer flavor.
  • Try truffle oil or cooked chicken as add-ins.
  • For vegan option, substitute butter and Parmesan with plant-based alternatives.

Serving Suggestions

Serve with a simple green salad and a glass of crisp white wine. This risotto pairs beautifully with roasted vegetables or grilled chicken.

Fun Fact

The word “risotto” comes from the Italian word “riso” meaning rice, and the technique relies on slowly cooking the rice in broth until it reaches a creamy consistency.
