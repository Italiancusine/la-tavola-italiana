Mushroom Risotto

Creamy Mushroom Risotto

Prep Time: 10 minutes

Cook Time: 30-40 minutes

Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 300g (10 oz) mushrooms (button, cremini, or a mix), sliced
  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine (optional, but recommended for depth of flavor)
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth, kept warm
  • 1/2 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)
  • Optional: A squeeze of lemon juice for a touch of brightness

Instructions:

  1. Prep the Ingredients: Start by chopping the onion and garlic, and slicing your mushrooms. It’s important to have everything ready because risotto requires attention as it cooks.
  2. Sauté the Aromatics: In a large pan or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté for about 3-4 minutes until softened. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  3. Cook the Mushrooms: Add the sliced mushrooms to the pan. Cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the mushrooms release their moisture and become golden brown.
  4. Toast the Rice: Stir in the Arborio rice and cook for 2-3 minutes, allowing the rice to lightly toast in the oil.
  5. Deglaze with Wine: If using white wine, pour it in now, scraping up any bits stuck to the bottom of the pan. Stir constantly until the wine is mostly absorbed by the rice, which should only take about 2 minutes.
  6. Add the Broth Gradually: Begin adding the warm broth, one ladle at a time, stirring constantly. Wait until most of the liquid has been absorbed before adding more broth. Continue this process for about 18-20 minutes. The rice should be tender and creamy, but still with a slight bite in the center (al dente).
  7. Finish with Butter and Parmesan: Once the rice is cooked to your desired texture, stir in the butter and grated Parmesan cheese. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Serve and Garnish: Spoon the creamy mushroom risotto onto plates and garnish with freshly chopped parsley. For an extra burst of flavor, you can add a squeeze of lemon juice.

Chef's Tips:

  • Broth Temperature: Make sure the broth is kept warm on the stove while you're cooking the risotto. Cold broth can slow down the cooking process and affect the texture of the rice.
  • Stirring Constantly: You don’t need to stir continuously, but stirring frequently ensures that the rice releases its starch and becomes nice and creamy.
  • Vegan Option: For a dairy-free version, you can use a plant-based butter and nutritional yeast instead of Parmesan.
  • Mushroom Varieties: Feel free to use a mix of mushrooms like shiitake, oyster, or porcini for an even deeper flavor profile.
  • Add-ins: You can add cooked chicken, spinach, or even truffle oil to elevate this dish.
