Aperol Spritz

Introduction

The Aperol Spritz is a refreshing Italian cocktail made with Prosecco, Aperol, and soda water. It’s fizzy, slightly bitter, and perfect for warm afternoons.

History

This drink originated in the Veneto region and became popular after World War II. Aperol, introduced in 1919, found its home in this modern classic.

Why We Love It

It’s bright, vibrant, and simple to prepare. The Aperol Spritz is the ultimate aperitivo to enjoy with friends or before a hearty meal.

Ingredients

  • 3 parts Prosecco
  • 2 parts Aperol
  • 1 part soda water
  • Orange slice (for garnish)
  • Ice cubes

Instructions

  1. Fill a wine glass generously with ice cubes.
  2. Pour in the Prosecco, then add Aperol.
  3. Top with soda water.
  4. Stir gently and garnish with an orange slice.

Tips & Tricks

  • Use chilled ingredients to keep the drink crisp.
  • Adjust Prosecco and Aperol ratio to taste—less Aperol for less bitterness.

Serving Suggestions

Serve before dinner with salty snacks like olives, cheese boards, or chips. It also pairs wonderfully with light Italian appetizers.

Nutrition Info (per serving)

  • Calories: 160
  • Sugar: 8g
  • Alcohol content: ~11%

Fun Fact

The Spritz has Austrian roots! It began when Austrian soldiers added water to strong Italian wine—and eventually evolved into this bubbly cocktail.
