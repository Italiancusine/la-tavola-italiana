Spaghetti Carbonara

Prep time: 10 mins | Cook time: 15 mins | Total time: 25 mins | Serves: 4

Ingredients

  • 400g (14 oz) spaghetti
  • 150g (5 oz) pancetta or guanciale, diced
  • 3 large eggs
  • 75g (3/4 cup) grated Pecorino Romano cheese
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Salt, for pasta water

Instructions

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the spaghetti until al dente according to package instructions.
  2. While the pasta cooks, heat a large skillet over medium heat. Add the pancetta or guanciale and cook until crisp. Remove from heat.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, grated Pecorino Romano, and a generous amount of black pepper.
  4. Reserve 1 cup of the pasta cooking water, then drain the spaghetti.
  5. Add the hot pasta to the skillet with pancetta and stir quickly to coat in the rendered fat.
  6. Remove from heat and quickly mix in the egg and cheese mixture, stirring vigorously to create a creamy sauce. Add reserved pasta water a little at a time to loosen the sauce if needed.
  7. Serve immediately with extra cheese and black pepper on top.

Tips

  • Use freshly grated Pecorino Romano for the most authentic flavor.
  • Do not add the eggs over direct heat to avoid scrambling.
  • Guanciale is traditional, but pancetta is a fine substitute.
