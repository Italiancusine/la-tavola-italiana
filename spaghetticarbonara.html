Introduction

Spaghetti Carbonara is a quintessential Roman pasta dish known for its creamy texture and rich, savory flavor. It's made with a few simple ingredients: eggs, cheese, pancetta, and pepper. Despite its simplicity, mastering carbonara takes attention to detail, especially to avoid scrambled eggs. The result, however, is a comforting, elegant dish that represents the soul of Italian home cooking.

History

The origins of carbonara are widely debated, but many agree that it gained popularity during World War II. Some believe it was born out of American soldiers combining their rations of eggs and bacon with Italian pasta. Others argue it’s a traditional Roman dish that simply evolved over time. Either way, it is now firmly rooted in Italy's culinary tradition.

Why We Love It

We love Spaghetti Carbonara for its silky, rich sauce made without cream, relying solely on eggs, cheese, and pancetta for deep, satisfying flavor. It’s quick to prepare, comforting, and perfect for any occasion.

Ingredients

  • 400g (14 oz) spaghetti
  • 150g (5 oz) pancetta or guanciale, diced
  • 2 whole eggs and 2 egg yolks
  • 1 cup grated Pecorino Romano cheese
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt (for pasta water)

Instructions

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the spaghetti until al dente.
  2. While the pasta cooks, fry the pancetta in a pan over medium heat until it is crispy. Set aside.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, yolks, cheese, and a generous amount of black pepper.
  4. Drain the pasta, reserving about 1 cup of the pasta water. Quickly add the hot pasta to the egg mixture, tossing to create a creamy sauce.
  5. Mix in the pancetta and a splash of pasta water if needed to loosen the sauce. Serve immediately with extra cheese and pepper.

Tips & Tricks

  • Use guanciale for a more traditional flavor, though pancetta is a good substitute.
  • The key to carbonara is using the heat of the pasta—not direct heat—to cook the eggs into a silky sauce.
  • Reserve enough pasta water to adjust the sauce's consistency as needed.
  • Do not add cream—authentic carbonara is creamy from the eggs and cheese alone.
  • Use freshly cracked black pepper for a punch of aroma and heat.

Serving Suggestions

Serve with a fresh green salad and a glass of crisp Italian white wine. Garlic bread on the side complements the rich flavors well.
